Who Is Bottling Plastic Pollution?

Plastic pollution harms the environment, wildlife, human health, and incurs significant economic costs. This project aims to explore global trends in plastic pollution and identify the countries that generate, recycle, and mismanage the most plastic. To tackle plastic pollution, we have to know where it is coming from. Only then can we raise awareness of the issue and guide effective strategies for reducing it.

Plastic in the Ocean

The Philippines contributes most to ocean plastic, in part, due to the large number of islands. It is therefore no surprise that the North Pacific contains the most plastic of all the oceans (home to the Great Pacific garbage patch). Globally, the equivalent of one garbage truck of plastic waste is dumped in the ocean every minute!

What are the Trends?

Charts 4 and 5 explore how development and income are related to plastic waste generation and pollution. They show that although high-income countries tend to manage waste better, they also tend to generate more. A $20 000 increase in GDP per capita is associated with 4.7kg less mismanaged plastic a year per person but an increase of 0.04kg of plastic waste generated per person per day.

How much are high-income countries to blame for exporting their plastic waste?

It is no secret that many high-income countries export their plastic waste. The UK exports 60% of theirs! In the past, the majority of plastic waste exports ended up in low/middle-income countries, increasing the likelihood it is mismanaged. Is it Possible to Predict Plastic Pollution?

I used sklearn inside python and applied a supervised machine learning algorithm to predict how much plastic waste each country dumps in the Ocean, using variables such as plastic waste generation, population, development etc that I collected from several different sources.

Colab

The predicted and tested pollution had an R-squared of 0.704, which shows the two variables are highly correlated. However, more variables are essential for more accurate predictions, such as the length of coastline, laws and regulations, which all require further research. I did have to bluntly force the predictions to be greater than or equal to zero, as a couple were initially negative.

Conclusion

Rapidly increasing production of plastic products is overwhelming the world's ability to manage it. Although high-income countries generate much more plastic waste, they pollute far less because they have more effective waste management systems. It is important to note that plastic pollution is a complex global problem that will require a multi-faceted approach to solve. Further research is essential to find effective policies, to start they should be targeted towards helping low-to-middle-income countries to improve waste management and regulation. Increasing awareness of plastic pollution and discouraging the use of single-use plastic will also play a vital role.
